KFW STUDENT LOAN — SAMPLE INFORMATION DOCUMENT
===============================================

PRODUCT: KfW Student Loan (Studienkredit)
PROVIDER: KfW Bankengruppe, Germany
VERSION: November 2023

--- OVERVIEW ---
The KfW Student Loan helps students finance their living costs during their studies in Germany. It is a flexible, low-interest loan available to domestic and international students enrolled at German universities.

--- LOAN AMOUNT ---
Minimum monthly payment: €100
Maximum monthly payment: €650
Maximum total loan amount: €54,600
Payments are made monthly directly to the student's bank account.

--- ELIGIBILITY ---
- Enrolled at a state-recognised German university or university of applied sciences
- Age: between 18 and 44 years old at time of application
- EU citizens and German residents are eligible
- Non-EU students may apply if they have been residing in Germany for at least 3 years
- Must not have already completed a Master's degree or equivalent

--- INTEREST RATE ---
The interest rate is variable and based on the 6-month EURIBOR plus a fixed margin.
Current indicative rate: approximately 7.18% per annum (as of Nov 2023) — this varies.
Interest is charged from the date each monthly payment is disbursed.

--- REPAYMENT ---
Repayment begins 6 to 23 months after the disbursement phase ends (student chooses).
Minimum repayment period: 5 years
Maximum repayment period: 25 years
Monthly repayments cover principal and interest.
Early full repayment is possible at any time without penalty.

--- DISBURSEMENT PERIOD ---
Maximum disbursement period: 14 semesters (7 years)
Payments stop automatically when the maximum is reached or the student leaves university.

--- APPLICATION ---
Applications are submitted online via the KfW website: www.kfw.de/studienkredit
The bank handles the contract; KfW manages the loan.
Processing time: approximately 4 to 8 weeks.

--- CANCELLATION & CHANGES ---
Students may cancel or reduce monthly payments with 1 month notice.
Payments can be paused for up to 2 years during the study phase (once).

--- EXCLUSIONS & IMPORTANT NOTES ---
- The loan is not available for part-time students
- Students who have exceeded the standard study period (Regelstudienzeit) by more than 4 semesters may not qualify
- The loan must be repaid regardless of employment status after graduation
- KfW does not forgive the loan in case of financial hardship (unlike some state grants)
- Subsidised BAföG grants are separate and do not affect KfW loan eligibility
